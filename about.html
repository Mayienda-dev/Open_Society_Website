Our Background

Open Society and Transformative Organization (OSTORG) is a Community-Based Organization founded in Kisii County, Kenya, and registered in 2025.

Our overall goal is to provide holistic support and empowerment to children and young people who are survivors of abuse, neglect, and exploitation so that they live a life of dignity, reach their full potential, develop their personal talents, and thrive for a safer and bright future.

Our Location

OSTORG main offices are based in Keroka town and at its rescue centre at Amasege, in the outskirts of Keroka town.

Keroka town is a major transit point which connects Kisii, Nyamira, Transmara, Bomet, and Kericho Counties to the rest of Kenya and neighbouring East African countries.

Strategic Position

Our strategic location allows us to serve multiple communities and respond to the growing needs of vulnerable children and youth in the region.

The Challenge We Address

Keroka town is teeming with children and young people who are steadily sliding into delinquency, majorly due to negative urban influence.

Rising Issues

  • Increasing number of street children
  • Rising school drop-out rates
  • Growing teenage pregnancy cases
  • Youth delinquency on the rise

Root Causes

  • Hard economic realities
  • Dysfunctional families
  • Child neglect and abuse
  • Substance and alcohol consumption
The Need for Early Intervention

There is an urgent need for early intervention to rescue these children and young people through protection, care, psycho-social support, and facilitating their access to basic needs, education, and training for the common good of all.

Our Approach

Community-Based Organization

As a registered Community Based Organization, we have opened our doors to needy children and youth in our midst (within Keroka town and environs).

Comprehensive Support

We serve orphans, teenage mothers, victims of dysfunctional families, and those neglected and abused in different ways.

Rehabilitation & Reintegration

We aim to offer them a safe haven for rehabilitation and reintegration to the mainstream society.

Educational Partnerships

We partner with educational institutions in our neighborhood to support these groups acquire education and skills for meaningful employment.
