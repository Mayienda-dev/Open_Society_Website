Our Objectives

Safe Shelter & Essential Services

Provide safe shelter and essential services including food, medical care, and counseling to those in need.

Skills Training & Education

Offer skills training and education programs for sustainable livelihoods and future independence.

Psychosocial Support

Facilitate access to justice and comprehensive psychosocial support for healing and recovery.

Community Partnerships

Foster community partnerships to prevent abuse and promote protection of vulnerable groups.

Rights Advocacy

Advocate for rights and policies supporting vulnerable groups at local and county government levels.

Who We Serve

OSTORG focuses on vulnerable children and young people who are neglected, abused, and exploited:

Orphans

Children who have lost one or both parents and need care, protection, and support to build a brighter future.

Teenage Mothers

Young mothers who need support, education, and skills to provide for themselves and their children.

Victims of Dysfunctional Families

Children from broken homes who need stability, counseling, and a supportive environment.

Neglected & Abused Children

Those from deprived backgrounds who have experienced neglect, abuse, or exploitation.

Scope of Our Work

Children's Rights as a Priority

OSTORG, as a Community-Based Organization, holds children and young people's rights as one of its key objectives. We believe that early intervention will rescue vulnerable children and young people in Kisii and Nyamira Counties, making a significant difference in their lives.

Holistic Support Services

We provide comprehensive support including:

  • Basic needs: food, clothing, and shelter
  • Medical care and health services
  • Psycho-social support and counseling
  • Talent development programs
  • Skills training and vocational education
  • Access to formal education

Transformative Impact

By offering holistic support to children and young people in Kisii and Nyamira County, OSTORG enhances their personal well-being and resilience, securing a safer and brighter future for themselves and their families. This transformation extends to communities in many different ways.

Additional Community Programs

Environmental Conservation

Training families on:

  • Kitchen gardening for food security
  • Growing environmentally friendly tree species
  • Use of organic manure
  • Safe disposal of solid waste

Impact: Neighboring communities benefit from adequate food for themselves and for sale to urban dwellers, while promoting environmental health.

Community Health Centre

Partnership plans with county government:

  • Run a health centre/dispensary within the rescue centre
  • Serve vulnerable children
  • Provide healthcare to the entire community
  • Member-donated residence for health facility

Benefit: Accessible healthcare for both rescue centre residents and the broader community.

Integration with Government Programs

Collaborative Approach

OSTORG implements its programs alongside other community development initiatives run by county and national government, including healthcare, education, skills training, and talent development. Our unique focus remains on vulnerable children and young people who are neglected, abused, and exploited.

Annual Budget

OSTORG's initial annual budget is approximately forty-five thousand USD ($45,000), dedicated to providing comprehensive support and services to vulnerable children and youth in our community.
